# ContextPulse — Vision Statement

**ContextPulse is the context layer that makes AI agents smarter every day.**

Your AI tools start every session blind — no memory of what you did yesterday, no idea what's on your screen, no awareness of what you just said. You're the bridge between your AI and your reality, manually screenshotting, copy-pasting, and re-explaining context that should already be there.

ContextPulse fixes this. It captures what you see, remembers what you've done, hears what you say, and coordinates your AI tools — all locally, all privately, all without you lifting a finger. And it gets better every day, learning from every interaction to deliver the right context at the right time.

**One platform. Four products. Each works alone. Together, they compound.**

---

## The Ecosystem

### ContextPulse Sight — Your AI gets eyes.
Always-on screen capture that runs invisibly in your system tray. Your AI agents see what you see — every frame, every window, every change — through 10 MCP tools. Under 1% CPU. Under 20MB RAM. The sensor layer.

### ContextPulse Memory — Your AI gets a brain.
Cross-session persistent memory that accumulates over time. Decisions, preferences, corrections, and project context that survive across sessions and across agents. The learning layer.

### ContextPulse Voice — Your AI gets ears.
Local voice-to-text that understands developer context. Dictate to your agents, transcribe meetings, capture spoken decisions. Self-improving — learns your vocabulary, fixes errors before they reach the LLM. The input layer.

### ContextPulse Agent — Your AI gets teammates.
Multi-agent coordination so Claude Code, Cursor, and Gemini CLI work as a team instead of isolated silos. Session handoff, shared state, conflict detection, and workflow orchestration. The coordination layer.

## Accessibility — Context for Everyone

ContextPulse already captures screens, extracts text via OCR, and processes voice. Reversing the direction — reading the screen *back* to the user — makes it a powerful accessibility tool.

- **Screen narration for low vision** — OCR the screen, describe what's there in natural language, read it aloud via TTS. Not robotic screen-reader style ("button, text field, link") but context-aware: "You're on line 47 of auth.py. There's a TypeError on line 52 — the function expects a string but got None."
- **Developer-aware descriptions** — understands IDEs, terminals, browser DevTools, and multi-monitor setups. Knows the difference between a code editor and a settings dialog.
- **Voice-controlled development** — combined with Voice, a developer with motor disabilities could navigate, dictate code, and get screen feedback entirely hands-free.
- **Adaptive capture** — for users with partial vision, auto-enhance captures with higher contrast, larger text rendering, or focused region zoom.

The accessibility market for developers is underserved. Traditional screen readers don't understand developer tools. ContextPulse could be the first context-aware accessibility layer built specifically for people who code.

## What We Believe

1. **Context is the bottleneck, not intelligence.** AI models are smart enough. They just don't know what you're looking at.
2. **Local-first is the only way.** Screen recordings, voice data, and work patterns are deeply private. Cloud-dependent tools will always hit a trust ceiling.
3. **Compounding beats features.** A tool that gets 1% better every day beats a tool with 100 features that never learns.
4. **Composable beats monolithic.** Developers want to install what they need, not buy an all-or-nothing platform.
5. **The best context is invisible.** If the user has to manually provide context, the product has failed.
