# ContextPulse Voice Guide

## Tone
Direct, technical, and confident without being cold. We talk to developers like a sharp colleague — no fluff, no marketing speak, no exclamation marks. We respect the reader's time and intelligence.

## Principles
- Be direct — say what it does, not what it "empowers you to unlock"
- Use active voice — "ContextPulse captures your screen" not "your screen is captured by ContextPulse"
- Technical accuracy over simplification — our audience knows what MCP, CLI, and env vars are
- Show, don't tell — a code snippet beats a paragraph of description
- Acknowledge tradeoffs honestly — if something has a limitation, say so

## Vocabulary

### Use
- "context" (not "data" or "information")
- "capture" (not "take a picture" or "snapshot")
- "agent" (not "bot" or "assistant" when referring to AI tools)
- "buffer" (technical, accurate)
- "blocklist" (not "blacklist")
- "always-on" (core value prop)
- "privacy-first"
- "daemon" (for the background process, developer audience gets this)

### Avoid
- "AI-powered" (we're infrastructure *for* AI, not AI ourselves)
- "revolutionary" / "game-changing" / superlatives
- "seamless" / "seamlessly"
- "leverage" (as a verb)
- "utilize" (just say "use")
- "smart" (vague — say what it actually does)
- Exclamation marks in product copy

## Example Copy

### Tagline
> Always-on context for AI agents

### One-liner (for README / PyPI)
> ContextPulse Screen captures your desktop continuously and serves it to AI coding assistants via MCP. Your AI can see what you see.

### Error Message
> Capture skipped — active window matches your privacy blocklist.

### Onboarding Welcome (system tray tooltip)
> ContextPulse Screen is running. Ctrl+Shift+S for a quick capture, or let auto-capture handle it.

### Feature Description
> Auto-capture takes a screenshot every 5 seconds, but only stores frames that actually changed. Your buffer stays lean — no duplicate frames eating disk space.
